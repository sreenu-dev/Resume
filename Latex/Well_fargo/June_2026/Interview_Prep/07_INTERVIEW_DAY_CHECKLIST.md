## 📝 During Interview - Question Handling

### When Asked a Question
1. **Pause and think** (2-3 seconds)
   - Don't rush to answer
   - Gather your thoughts
   - Plan your response

2. **Ask clarifying questions** (if needed)
   - "Can you clarify what you mean by...?"
   - "Are there any constraints I should consider?"
   - "What's the expected time complexity?"

3. **Provide your answer**
   - Use STAR method for behavioral questions
   - Discuss approach for technical questions
   - Be concise but complete

4. **Ask follow-up questions**
   - "Does this approach make sense?"
   - "Would you like me to optimize this further?"
   - "Do you have any questions about my solution?"

### If You Don't Know the Answer
✅ **Do:**
- Be honest
- Explain your thought process
- Ask for hints
- Try to figure it out
- Learn from feedback

❌ **Don't:**
- Lie or make up answers
- Panic or get defensive
- Give up immediately
- Blame others
- Ignore feedback

### If You Make a Mistake
✅ **Do:**
- Acknowledge the mistake
- Explain how to fix it
- Learn from it
- Move forward
- Stay positive

❌ **Don't:**
- Panic or get defensive
- Make excuses
- Blame others
- Give up
- Dwell on it

## 💬 Questions to Ask Interviewer

### Technical Questions
1. "What is the current tech stack for this team?"
2. "What are the biggest technical challenges you're facing?"
3. "How do you approach system design?"
4. "What development practices do you follow?"
5. "How do you handle security and compliance?"

### Team & Culture Questions
1. "How would you describe the team culture?"
2. "What does a typical day look like?"
3. "How large is the team?"
4. "What opportunities are there for growth?"
5. "How does Wells Fargo support professional development?"

### Role-Specific Questions
1. "What would success look like in the first 90 days?"
2. "What are the biggest challenges in this role?"
3. "Who would I be working with?"
4. "What's the career progression?"

### Company Questions
1. "What's the company's vision for the next 5 years?"
2. "How is Wells Fargo adapting to fintech competition?"
3. "What are the strategic priorities?"

## 📞 After Interview

### Immediately After Interview
- [ ] Take a moment to decompress
- [ ] Reflect on your performance
- [ ] Note areas that went well
- [ ] Note areas for improvement
- [ ] Don't be too hard on yourself

### Within 24 Hours
- [ ] Send thank you email
  - [ ] Mention specific points from conversation
  - [ ] Reiterate your interest
  - [ ] Keep it brief and professional
  - [ ] Proofread before sending

### Sample Thank You Email
```
Subject: Thank You - [Your Name] - [Position Title]

Dear [Interviewer Name],

Thank you for taking the time to interview me today for the [Position Title] 
role. I really enjoyed our conversation about [specific topic discussed].

Your insights about [specific point] were particularly valuable and reinforced 
my interest in the role. I'm excited about the opportunity to contribute to 
[specific project/goal].

I'm confident that my experience in [relevant skill] would be valuable to your 
team. Please let me know if you need any additional information from me.

Thank you again for the opportunity.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Phone Number]
[Your Email]
```

### Follow-up Timeline
- **Day 1:** Send thank you email
- **Day 5:** If no response, send light follow-up
- **Day 10:** If no response, send another follow-up
- **Day 14:** If no response, assume rejection and move on
