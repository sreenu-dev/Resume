## Resume Transformation Strategy

### Rule 1: Every Bullet = Customer Impact
Amazon interviewers ask: **"Who was the customer? How were they helped?"**

**Before (Wells Fargo framing):**
> "Reduced system error ratio by 70% with comprehensive audit logging for compliance tracking."

**After (Amazon framing):**
> "Drove a 70% reduction in system error ratio, directly improving reliability for 50,000+ end users by redesigning API response schemas and introducing structured error telemetry."

---

### Rule 2: Signal Ownership Everywhere
Amazon wants to see you *owned* outcomes — not just contributed.

**Before:**
> "Collaborated with teams to implement caching layers using Redis."

**After:**
> "Owned end-to-end design and delivery of a Redis caching layer, driving a 22% boost in system throughput and reducing downstream DB pressure by 40%."

---

### Rule 3: Think Big on Scale
Amazon operates at internet scale. Make your numbers bigger and more explicit.

**Before:**
> "Maintained 99.9% production uptime."

**After:**
> "Maintained 99.9% production uptime across a system serving 100,000+ concurrent sessions, proactively resolving critical cross-stack incidents to prevent customer-facing degradation."

---

### Rule 4: Add AWS to Your Stack Now
Even if you've used Azure, add AWS services you know or have used in projects. If you haven't, **learn the basics in 1 week** — this is non-negotiable for Amazon.

**Priority AWS Services to Learn:**
- **EC2** (Virtual machines) — 2 hours
- **S3** (Object storage) — 2 hours
- **Lambda** (Serverless) — 3 hours
- **RDS / DynamoDB** (Databases) — 4 hours
- **SQS / SNS** (Messaging) — 3 hours
- **API Gateway** (Already know the concept) — 2 hours
- **CloudWatch** (Monitoring) — 2 hours
- **ECS / EKS** (Containers — you know K8s) — 3 hours

**Total: ~21 hours of focused learning**
